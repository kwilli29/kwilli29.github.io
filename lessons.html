Lessons Learned

  1. Get your visa sorted out as early as possible. Like 6 months in advanced. It'll somehow never be early enough to start getting things organized and sent in but you can at least try.
  2. Have comfortable walking shoes. There's so much walking and so many stairs here I'm dying.
  3. Don't succumb to the fatigue you get at 5pm. You will have to participate in a Spaghetti-Western-style showdown with death itself and you will only realize you lost when you wake up at 5am the next day.
  4. Be patient. You don't have to do something exciting every single day. Sometimes listening to a podcast and watching the sunset from a nearby park is all you need to enjoy your day.
  5. McDonald's at 5am after being up all night HITS. But also yeah don't stay up all night; it's not worth it. That wasn't something learned, rather it was something I recently experienced for the first time.
  6. Eating alone at a restaurant is a lovely experience, but I also don't know what to do when I'm eating. I usually wait until I get all my food, in case the staff needs to say something to me, take a quick picture of the food, and then I listen to my podcast and enjoy the food.
  7. Someone on the rugby team taught me how to properly use chopsticks for the first time in my life. Being a self-taught chopstick user has hindered my progress. It was funny trying to adjust. I could pick up small pieces of chicken, but have not yet graduated to noodles. Those are a bit more slippery and advanced for my current skill level.
  8. I learned how to properly pass in rugby. Technically a lesson learned even though I had played 3 seasons of it before this year. Passing was always my greatest weakness. It's improved a lot though!
  9. Home is where the grocery store you can wander around in is.
  10. I REALLY hate bugs.
  11. Don't be afraid to assert your right to leave a social event when your social battery has run well past being empty.
