Victoria Peak

There are 2 routes to the top of Victoria Peak near my flats. One is a paved, steady inclined that is perfect for families to take up to the peak - it's hardly even a hike one could argue. We (me and my flatmate) did not take that path up. The path we took to the top was definitely more like a real hike. Dirt paths, endless stone stairs to help with the steeper inclines, and humidity is what we were met with. It was really fun though, I don't get to hike much in the Midwest and it makes me really excited to explore more of the trails they have here in Hong Kong. There were restaraunts toward the top so when we had dinner it was a pretty incredible view. Nothing like a spur of the moment hike to the top of a small mountain.

Arcade Night

Some of the exchange students set up an arcade night in Kowloon. It was filled with claw machines, bright lights, and plushies. We poured more money than I'd like to admit on these 2 big plushies - these claw machine games are truly scams but the rush of winning is like no other. I even won a mini plushie on my first try at one of the smaller claw machines. It was more exhilarating than the horse races.

BIG Buddha

The last trip I made was to the famous Big Buddha on Lantau Island. We took a cable car up to the statue and spent the afternoon exploring all the sights to see. I was amazed at all the symbolism of the statue's design; I was also surprised to find out it was built in the 1980s. The island was beautiful, the monastery was incredible, and the Buddha was big. Our group ate some tasty Indonesian food afterwards. Overall a wonderful time.
